Hiking, Camping, and Outdoor Activites

Yeah, it can kinda be grouped in traveling, and yeah, it's a pretty basic hobby, but I find there's nothing more freeing than getting lost. When I was staying in the Daintree Rainforest, I parked my rental car at a carpark and found a unpaved trail nearby, claiming to be a .5km, 30-minute long hike... 90 minutes later, I'm alone and confused, cheap sandals on my feet, in the middle of the jungle, following a 6-inch wide, barely beaten-down path winding its way up and down ravines, over creeks, and further and further away from civilization. It was only when I stopped and took a look around that I realized: "this is what I've been chasing". Totally alone, no echo of highways and people, just me and the Earth. I heard animals and noises I had never heard before, witnessed nature in its truest form, and even spotted some cassowaries, an elusive, dinosaur-era bird native to Australia's rainforests. It's the only time I've ever that overpowering sense of isolation, and it's a feeling I cherish and refernece often in my daily life.
